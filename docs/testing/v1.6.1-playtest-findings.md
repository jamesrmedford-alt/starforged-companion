## F1 — Session toolbar button didn't render on Foundry v13 build 350 · 🟢 resolved (external)

**Observed:** The ▶ Session button (`sfSession`) did not appear in the Token

v13 pattern (`getSceneControlButtons` registers metadata, `renderSceneControls`
binds the click) in `src/index.js`, and `sfSession` has **no `visible` gate**, so
it should show for all users. The v1.6.1 commits only touched comment text in
`src/index.js`, never the toolbar code.

**For later:** if it recurs on a specific v13 build, investigate whether the
first-registered tool or a tool lacking an explicit `order` is dropped by that
build's scene-controls renderer.

## Theme T1 — an explicit "entity created & assigned" lifecycle gate · 🟡 candidate direction

Across F3 / F4 / F6 / F7 a pattern emerges: the module **generates names, flavor,
art, and journal/Notes stubs eagerly at creation time**, before the entity is
named, assigned, and carrying its real details (assets, type, sheet fields). The
result is placeholder-name references ("Ship") and invented details (fighters,
oversized engines, off-sector settlements) that don't match the finished entity.

**Reporter's framing:** *"I'm starting to feel like we need to have a process for
declaring the ship 'created' and assigned."*

**Candidate direction (not yet actioned):** an explicit "entity finalized" step —
analogous to the Session **Begin** gate — so generation (flavor / art / Notes /
stub) fires **after** naming + assignment and is **grounded in the actual sheet
data**. Relates to `entity-actor-migration-scope.md` (native Actors as the source
of truth) and the generate-once/locked portrait lifecycle in `../decisions.md`.

**Confirmed behavior (not a bug):** a starship carrying the **Command Vehicle**
asset shows an **INTEGRITY** meter (5 → 0) on its sheet; modules (e.g. Research
Lab) appear as their own asset cards.

## F11 — Two-voice NPC audio plays on top of itself (segments overlap) · 🔴 open

**Environment:** narrator card with an NPC line (Vance), audio narration enabled,
two voices configured.

**Positive:** first successful **two-voice split** — the narrator voice and a
distinct NPC voice were both produced, so the `<npc>` audio markup did its job on
the synthesis side.

**Bug:** the two audio segments **played simultaneously** (overlapping) rather than
one after the other. Playback is *designed* as a sequential queue —
`src/audio/playback.js` docstring: "the queue plays in order; each `end` advances
to the next" — so the `end` → advance chaining isn't holding here; both segments
start at once.

**Hypothesis (to verify):** playback *is* a sequential `while`-loop that awaits each
segment (`src/audio/playback.js:199–206`, `_playFromCurrent` → `_playOneSound`), so
the overlap implies one of: (a) `_playOneSound` resolves on `play()`-start rather
than on the segment's `end` event, advancing the loop early; (b) the `end` event
isn't firing on this Forge/v13 build (same `Sound` event-listener family as
**AUDIO-001** in `known-issues.md`); or (c) two playback sessions started
concurrently (e.g. autoplay + a manual click, or a double-bound play button in
`src/audio/index.js`). Code: `src/audio/playback.js` + `src/audio/index.js`.

**Relation:** same `<npc>` markup as **F9a** — F9a is its *display* side (tags
leaking into the card), F11 is the *audio* side (segment ordering).

## F12 — Burn momentum turned a Weak Hit into a Miss · 🔴 open · ⚠️ HIGH (mechanical / play-affecting)

**This is the first mechanical bug** (it changes a roll outcome), not a
cosmetic/timing one.

**Environment:** Face Danger. Original roll **Action 5 + 2 = 7** vs **Challenge
[6, 7]** → **Weak Hit** (7 beats 6, ties 7). Player clicked **Burn momentum** →
card flipped to **"Burned → Miss" / "Pay the Price."**

**Reporter's key clue:** *"the action score and challenge score were the same as the
original roll"* — the card still showed **5 + 2 = 7** vs **6, 7**, but labeled it a
**Miss**. 7 vs [6,7] is a **Weak Hit**, so the displayed numbers don't justify a Miss.

**Why this is wrong:** per the play kit (and `src/moves/burnMomentum.js:6–12`),
burning momentum can **only improve or hold** an outcome — never worsen it — and the
card is supposed to **only offer the burn when it helps**.

**Code facts (corrected after reading the source):**
- Real functions: `buildBurnState` (`burnMomentum.js:63`), `canBurnMomentum` /
  `applyMomentumBurn` (`src/moves/resolver.js:360` / `:369`). *(An earlier draft of
  this entry named `computeBurnOutcome` / `shouldOfferBurn` / `resolveBurnContext` —
  those do not exist; corrected here.)*
- The click handler burns the **stored** `burn.momentum` (`handleBurnClick`,
  `burnMomentum.js:187`) — the same `postMomentum` computed at render
  (`buildBurnState:67–69`). So render and click use the **same** momentum; the bug is
  **not** a render-vs-click mismatch.
- The eligibility gate `canBurnMomentum` (`resolver.js:360–367`) is just
  `return momentum > Math.min(c1, c2)` (plus not-progress / momentum>0 / not-already-
  strong-hit). **It compares momentum only to the lower challenge die — it never
  compares the *burned* outcome to the *current* outcome.** `applyMomentumBurn`
  (`:369–374`) is the standard `> both → strong / > one → weak / else miss`.

**Inconsistency to repro:** with the **displayed** dice **[6, 7]**, an *offered* burn
requires `momentum > 6` (≥ 7), and `applyMomentumBurn(7,[6,7])` → **weak_hit**, never
**miss** — yet the card shows **Miss**. So either the momentum/dice actually used at
burn differ from what the card displays, or the "Miss" label/consequence is applied
downstream incorrectly (`buildBurnedCardContent` / `mapConsequences`). **Needs a live
repro capturing the actor's real momentum value** to pin exactly.

**Defects:**
1. **Mechanical (HIGH):** `canBurnMomentum` lacks an explicit *"burned outcome ≥
   current outcome"* check (it only asks whether momentum beats the lower die), and
   `handleBurnClick` doesn't re-assert improvement before applying. A burn that
   doesn't strictly improve should be neither offered nor applied — add the guard in
   **both** `buildBurnState` and `handleBurnClick`.
2. **Display:** the move-result card shows the **original** action score (5+2=7) next
   to the burned label, so the numbers contradict the outcome. After a burn the card
   should surface the **momentum value** used as the score.

**Code:** `src/moves/resolver.js` (`canBurnMomentum` / `applyMomentumBurn`),
`src/moves/burnMomentum.js` (`buildBurnState` / `handleBurnClick` /
`buildBurnedCardContent`), `src/index.js` (burn render at `:899` / `:2248`),
`src/character/actorBridge.js` (momentum read).

## F13 — Burn posts a 2nd narrator card; both audios overlap · 🔴 open

**Environment:** continued from F12 — clicking **Burn Momentum** (this time the burn
*upgraded* the outcome). "−1 momentum (now 2)" meter card above.

**Observed:** two **Narrator** prose cards appeared for the one move —
- Card 1 (original outcome): *"…rows of sleek unmarked cases… your crowbar slips…
  a proximity sensor chirps twice… the cargo bay's external airlock just registered
  movement."* (the weak-hit complication)
- Card 2 (burned/upgraded outcome): *"…the real cargo—not medical supplies at all,
  but military-grade munitions… The manifest was a lie…"* (the cleaner result)

…and **both cards' audio played over each other** ("again" — same overlap class as
**F11**, now across two cards).

**F13a — duplicate, now-contradictory narration.** `renarrate()`
(`src/moves/burnMomentum.js:238–281`) always **posts a fresh narration card** for the
upgraded outcome via `narrate(synthResolution, …)`, while the **original** narration
card is left in place. So the superseded prose lingers — and it describes events the
burn undid (the airlock/proximity complication the upgrade removed). (The move-
*result* card is correctly rewritten in place by `message.update` +
`buildBurnedCardContent`; it's the separate *narration* card that duplicates.)
Consider superseding / striking the original narration card instead of appending a
second.

**F13b — cross-card audio overlap.** Both narration cards triggered playback at once
— the **two-concurrent-sessions** variant hypothesised in **F11(c)**. Strongly
suggests the audio layer needs a **global "stop any playing narration before starting
another"** singleton, which would also cover F11's within-card case. Code:
`src/audio/index.js` (togglePlayback / autoplay) + `src/audio/playback.js`.

**Relation:** F13 sits at the intersection of **F12** (the burn re-narration path)
and **F11** (audio overlap) — a burn fix should resolve the duplicate card *and* the
overlapping audio together.

## F16 — Foundry's "Starforged tools" group hides the Companion tools (no way back) · 🔴 open · usability

**Environment:** far-left scene controls. Clicking the (foundry-ironsworn system)
**"Starforged tools"** control group makes the **Starforged Companion** buttons
disappear, and they're "not recoverable."

**Cause:** the Companion injects its buttons into the **`tokens`** control group
(`controls.tokens.tools.*` in `getSceneControlButtons`, `src/index.js`), not its own
top-level group. Selecting any other group (the system's "Starforged tools", journal,
etc.) deactivates the tokens group and hides our tools, with no labeled way back.

**Reporter's fix (agreed direction):** give the Companion its **own top-level
scene-control group** (e.g. `controls.starforgedCompanion = { name, title, icon,
tools }`) so the buttons live in a dedicated, always-reachable category instead of
riding inside `tokens`. Code: `src/index.js` getSceneControlButtons / renderSceneControls.
